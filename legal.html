Privacy Policy

Last updated · June 2026

In plain language

Your individual health results belong to you. Employers only ever see aggregate, anonymised data — never an individual's results. We collect the minimum we need, encrypt it, and keep it only as long as necessary.

What we collect

Programme data (biomarker results, app activity, supplement adherence) tied to enrolled individuals, and limited account and contact information. Health data is handled as special-category personal data.

How we use it

  • To produce the individual's physician-signed Smart Report and plan
  • To deliver the LifeOS app experience and supplements
  • To produce aggregate, anonymised reporting for the employer

Your rights

You may access, correct, or request deletion of your personal data, and withdraw consent, subject to applicable law. Contact our Data Protection Officer at privacy@aiwo.com.

Cookie Policy

Last updated · June 2026

In plain language

We use essential cookies to run the site and, with your consent, privacy-friendly analytics to improve it. You control non-essential cookies.

Categories

  • Essential — required for the site to function
  • Analytics — privacy-first measurement, only with consent
  • Preferences — remember language and settings

You can change your cookie preferences at any time via the consent banner.

Terms of Use

Last updated · June 2026

In plain language

This site is for information about the AIWO Tribe programme. Nothing here is medical advice or a guarantee of outcomes — programme outcomes are measured trajectories.

Use of the site

Content is provided for general information. Programme specifics, inclusions and pricing are confirmed in a written proposal. Figures shown are indicative.

No medical advice

Information on this site does not constitute medical advice. The programme is physician-reviewed; clinical decisions are made by qualified clinicians, never by automated systems alone.

No guarantee

Outcomes are measured trajectories, not guarantees. Comparative benchmarks are attributed and clearly labelled.

DPDPA Notice

Last updated · June 2026

In plain language

We handle your health data under India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act: consent first, minimum necessary, secured, and never shared with your employer at an individual level.

Lawful basis & consent

Informed consent precedes every draw — a hard stop. Health data is processed as special-category personal data, with least-access controls and defined retention.

The employer boundary

  • Single-channel delivery of results to the individual
  • Aggregate-only to employers, with minimum-cohort suppression
  • Result-suppression enforced contractually and technically

Grievance & contact

For data-protection queries or grievances, contact our Data Protection Officer at privacy@aiwo.com.

Accessibility Statement

Last updated · June 2026

In plain language

We aim to meet WCAG 2.2 AA. The site supports keyboard navigation, visible focus, reduced motion, and accessible contrast. Tell us if anything falls short.

Our commitment

  • WCAG 2.2 AA as the target standard
  • Full keyboard navigation and visible focus states
  • Respects prefers-reduced-motion
  • Semantic structure, alt text and AA contrast

Found a barrier? Email accessibility@aiwo.com and we'll address it.

Security · Trust Centre

Last updated · June 2026

In plain language

We treat health data as the most sensitive category there is — encrypted, least-access, audited, and held to a recognised compliance roadmap.

Controls

  • Encryption in transit (TLS) and at rest
  • Role-based, least-privilege access with audit logging
  • DPDPA alignment; ISO 27001 & SOC-2 on the roadmap
  • Maintained sub-processor register

Responsible disclosure

If you believe you've found a vulnerability, please email security@aiwo.com. We commit to acknowledging reports and working with researchers in good faith. A data incident carries a red threshold of greater than zero.
