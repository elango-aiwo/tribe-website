Why a 90-day cycle beats an annual check-up

AL
The AIWO Longevity Team
Published June 2026 · Updated June 2026

The annual check-up is a ritual almost every organisation pays for and almost no one gets value from. A morning of tests, a PDF of results, a folder that is never opened again. Twelve months later, the same ritual, with no connection to the last one. It produces data, but never a direction.

The problem is not the testing. It is the cadence. Biology does not move on an annual schedule, and neither does behaviour change. Metabolic markers respond to intervention in weeks. Sleep, training and nutrition habits form or collapse in a quarter. An annual snapshot misses the entire feedback loop in which real change happens.

What a quarter can actually move

Ninety days is long enough for biology to respond and short enough for a person to stay engaged. Fasting glucose, triglycerides, inflammatory markers, sleep quality and body composition all show measurable movement inside a quarter when the intervention is specific. That is the window where effort and evidence can meet.

This is why AIWO Tribe runs on the SIIS cycle rather than an annual calendar: a perpetual 90-day loop of Signals, Interpretations, Interventions and Systems.

An annual check-up tells you where you were. A 90-day loop tells you whether what you are doing is working, while there is still time to change it.
Snapshot versus trajectory

The four stages of the loop

  • Signals: 200+ biomarkers across 7 panels establish where each person actually stands
  • Interpretations: a physician-signed Smart Report turns raw numbers into plain language and priority
  • Interventions: one prioritised plan across the 11+1 lifestyle pillars, guided daily in the LifeOS app
  • Systems: habits, cohorts and challenges that hold the change until the next re-measure

Why the cadence is the proof

The quarterly re-measure is what turns a wellbeing programme into an evidence engine. Every cycle produces a fresh reading of the same markers, so improvement is shown rather than assumed. Four cycles into the year, an employee has a personal trajectory, and the employer has an aggregate one: anonymised, board-legible, and honest about being a measured trajectory rather than a guarantee.

The annual check-up asked people to change everything and then looked away for a year. The 90-day cycle asks for one prioritised change at a time and checks the result while motivation is still alive. That difference in cadence, more than any single test or app feature, is why a re-measured loop moves biology when the yearly ritual never did.
